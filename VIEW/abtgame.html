MOBILE LEGENDS: BANG BANG

Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (MLBB) is a free-to-play multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed by Moonton in 2016, designed for mobile platforms like Android and iOS. It features fast-paced 5v5 battles where two teams compete across three lanes to destroy the enemy base, using a diverse roster of heroes with roles such as Tank, Marksman, Assassin, Mage, Fighter, and Support. With matches lasting around 10–20 minutes, the game emphasizes teamwork, strategy, and skill, making it accessible yet highly competitive. MLBB has achieved over 1 billion downloads and more than 100 million monthly active players, becoming a cultural phenomenon in Southeast Asia and a major force in global esports through leagues like the Mobile Legends Professional League (MPL) and the M-Series World Championships. It has even been recognized as an official medal event in the SEA Games and is set to debut in the Asian Games 2026, solidifying its place as one of the most influential mobile games in the world.

MLBB BATTLEFIELD MAP

There are three lanes on the map: Top Lane, Middle Lane (or Mid Lane) and Bottom Lane (or Bot Lane). All three lanes are set up with three turrets each and your goal is to get through waves of minions and destroy all the turrets. Once you have passed the turrets, you need to destroy enemy base building to win the game.

RANK SYSTEM

There are seven ranks in Mobile Legends: Bang Bang. These are: Warrior, Elite, Master, Grandmaster, Epic, Legend, and Mythic.

Each ranks has divisions written in Roman numerals and a number of stars required to be promoted to the next division or rank. If a player exceeds the maximum number of stars in their current division or falls below zero stars, they can advance to the next division or back to the previous division. Completing all divisions from their current rank or being demoted from all divisions will result in a promotion to the next rank or a demotion to the previous rank.

In Mythic, the stars have been replaced with points to advance to the next division.

DRAFT PICK

The main purpose of Draft Pick is to diversify the hero pool of a player and not rely only on a singular or few heroes they specialize in, instead, it forces the player to adjust and strategize amongst their teammates to win.

When a player reaches Epic V or higher (Grandmaster V or higher in Advanced Server), Draft Pick becomes available for future matches. Unlike Blind Pick, Draft Pick allows both teams to see their heroes while being picked, and allows for up to 10 banned heroes (3 / 4 / 5 bans at Epic / Legend / Mythic) for each team for balancing purposes. Heroes who have already been selected or banned cannot be picked for the duration of the match.

In Draft Pick, there are 3 phases: Banning Phase, Picking Phase, and Last Change Phase.

My Mobile Legends Experience

Playing Mobile Legends has been one of my favorite ways to relax and have fun. The matches are short but full of action, so even a quick game feels exciting. I enjoy picking different heroes — sometimes going aggressive as a Marksman, other times holding the line as a Assasin. What makes it special is the teamwork: those moments when everyone clicks and we pull off a perfect fight are the best. Of course, there are times when teammates don’t cooperate, but that’s part of the challenge and makes the wins feel even more rewarding. Beyond the game itself, watching MPL tournaments adds extra hype, inspiring me to try new strategies. For me, Mobile Legends isn’t just a game — it’s about the fun, the community, and the little victories that keep me coming back.
